WHERE CAN YOU FIND E.T.?

Tyler Guerrero, Emanuel Padilla, Cameron Snyder

Slide 1: Intro

A Peek Into the Life of the E.T. Link to UFO Sightings Around the World

The data is a CSV from Kaggle based on Extraterrestial Reports from 1910-????. It shows location, time, shapes of the extraterrestial, and brief descriptions of the event.

Slide 2: Problem and Questions

Where should you vacation to if you want a chance at meeting E.T? -Is there a country that best suits this need? -What time of year does E.T. prefer to travel? -Is there a particular time of day that E.T. leaves his house? -Could E.T. be among us at this very moment?

Slide 3: Background/Reason for Choosing Data

We always hear about controversies and tinfoil hats, but we were curious how often do those from space come to visit, if at all? Is Earth a local hotspot for E.T. and his friends? Is Coachella a galaxy-wide attraction? These questions felt fun to dive into and answer.

The following libraries were used to manipulate our data and create graphs: (ggplot2) (dplyr) (gridExtra) (grid) (RColorBrewer) (ggrepel) (ggthemes) (viridis) (ggridges) (tidyverse) (lubridate)

Top states and Seasons

season_state_ufo <- new_ufo %>%
  mutate(
    date = parse_date_time(date, orders = c("mdy", "ymd", "dmy", "mdy HM", "ymd HM")),
    month_num = month(date),
    season = case_when(
      month_num %in% c(12, 1, 2)  ~ "Winter",
      month_num %in% c(3, 4, 5)   ~ "Spring",
      month_num %in% c(6, 7, 8)   ~ "Summer",
      month_num %in% c(9, 10, 11) ~ "Fall"
    )
  ) %>%
  filter(country == "us") %>%        #focusing on the united states 
  count(season, state, name = "n") %>%
  group_by(season) %>%
  slice_max(n, n = 10)               #top 10 states per season

ggplot(season_state_ufo, aes(x = reorder(state, n), y = n, fill = season)) +
  geom_col(color="black", show.legend = FALSE) +
  coord_flip() +
  facet_wrap(~ season, scales = "free_y") +
  scale_fill_manual(values = c(
    "Winter" = "blue",
    "Spring" = "green",
    "Summer" = "red",
    "Fall"   = "yellow"
  )) +
  labs(
    title = "Top 10 States for UFO Sightings by Season in the United States",
    x = "State",
    y= "Number of Sightings"
  ) +
  theme_minimal()

slide: Day or Night

ca_summer_tod <- new_ufo %>%
  mutate(
    date = parse_date_time(date, orders = c("mdy", "ymd", "dmy")),
    hour= as.integer(substr(time, 1, 2)),  # getting the  hour from the time var string
    time_of_day = case_when(
      hour >= 6 & hour < 20~ "Daytime",
      TRUE~ "Nighttime"
    ),
    month_num   = month(date),
    season      = case_when(
      month_num %in% c(12, 1, 2) ~ "Winter",
      month_num %in% c(3, 4, 5)  ~ "Spring",
      month_num %in% c(6, 7, 8)  ~ "Summer",
      month_num %in% c(9,10,11)  ~ "Fall"
    )
  ) %>%
  filter(country == "us", state == "ca", season == "Summer") %>%
  count(time_of_day, name = "n")


ggplot(ca_summer_tod, aes(x = time_of_day, y = n, fill = time_of_day)) +
  geom_col(color = "black", show.legend = FALSE) +
  scale_fill_manual(values = c(
    "Daytime"   = "gold",
    "Nighttime" = "navy"
  )) +
  labs(
    title = "UFO Sightings by Time of Day in California (Summer)",
    x = "Time of Day",
    y= "Number of Sightings"
  ) +
  theme_minimal()
